A plump, orange-hued, rubbery clay block shaped like a capybara hovers above the construction site’s ground. Directly above its head rests a dense, slow-flowing cube of sand. Ahead, a pale yellow, elastic cylindrical rubber block is launched with horizontal velocity. Under gravity’s pull, all three descend together. The sand spreads across the clay’s head upon impact, while the rubber cylinder rebounds upward, colliding forcefully with the clay’s front. The resulting force fractures the soft clay body, splitting it apart as it settles into the dusty surface.
